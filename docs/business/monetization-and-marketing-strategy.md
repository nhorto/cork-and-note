# Cork & Note — Business Model, Monetization & Marketing Strategy

**Prepared:** June 2026
**Scope:** US market only · Target users: casual wine drinkers + winery/tasting-room visitors · Bootstrapped founder with a small marketing budget
**Method:** Multi-source web research across five workstreams (monetization, competitors, market context, marketing/UA, regulation), with adversarial cross-checking. Confidence levels and source conflicts are flagged throughout. Many vendor "market size" figures conflict by 2–10×; treat those as **directional only**. Higher-confidence figures come from OIV, Gallup, SVB, Sovos, WineAmerica, RevenueCat, and primary platform/legal sources.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**What Cork & Note is.** A wine app combining a personal cellar/collection manager, tasting-note logging, a **winery map for tasting-room visits**, a wishlist, wine browsing, and an **AI "Sommelier" chat**. That last-two combination — *visit logging + AI sommelier grounded in your own tastings* — is the strategic wedge.

**The market in one line.** The *liquid* (wine itself) is in a real, multi-year decline; the *tech wrapper* around wine (apps, e-commerce, AI discovery) is growing 11–34%/yr. Build for the tech tailwind, not the wine tailwind.

**The single biggest opportunity.** Every major incumbent — Vivino, CellarTracker, Wine-Searcher, Delectable, Hello Vino — has **no winery-visit / tasting-room logging**. That capability today lives in a fragmented, separate ecosystem of "passport" and booking apps (Winery Passport, state passports, CellarPass) that are **not** connected to personal cellars, ratings, or AI. **No one owns "Untappd-for-wineries + your cellar + an AI sommelier."** That is Cork & Note's defensible niche against Vivino's incumbency.

**Recommended monetization (detail in §3):**
- **Primary: Freemium subscription** ("Cork & Note Premium," target **~$4.99/mo or ~$29.99–39.99/yr**). This is the realistic, controllable revenue engine for a bootstrapped consumer app. Expect **~2–5% free-to-paid** conversion with a soft paywall, materially higher if you gate harder.
- **Secondary (later, opt-in): B2B winery/tasting-room partnerships** — sponsored map placements, featured-winery listings, and visit analytics. This monetizes the visit-logging wedge and aligns with an industry desperate for engagement tools.
- **Tertiary / defer: affiliate "buy this wine" commerce.** Real but legally heavy in US alcohol (three-tier/tied-house, state-by-state DTC) and thin-margin as a downstream affiliate (~2.5–6%). Pursue only after scale, and only via licensed-retailer partners.
- **Avoid as a primary model: banner ads** (US eCPM <$1 — negligible). Instead, *sell "ad-free" as a Premium perk* (the Vivino/Delectable move).

**Top 5 marketing moves to start with (small budget):**
1. **ASO** — your #1 free channel (~65% of app discovery is store search). Win long-tail keywords ("winery tasting room," "wine tasting notes," "scan wine label"). Cost: $0–80/mo.
2. **Founder-led #WineTok / Reels** — casual, personality-driven wine content with the app shown naturally. Cost: time. 40% of US wine consumers use TikTok weekly.
3. **Tasting-room QR partnerships** — your defensible edge: QR table-tents/cards at partner wineries that capture visits + tasting notes. Grassroots, high-intent, where you beat Vivino.
4. **Apple Search Ads** — once live, $50/day on brand + high-intent keywords (cheapest, highest-retention iOS install source, ~$1.40–3.00 CPI).
5. **5–15 wine micro/nano influencers** on gifted/affiliate + small flat fees, tracked with promo codes.

**The honest risk.** US wine consumption is down 52+ straight months, the drinker base is shrinking and getting more health-conscious, and Vivino is ~100× bigger than the #2 wine app. Cork & Note wins by being *narrow and differentiated* (the casual + tasting-room-visitor niche with an AI sommelier), not by competing with Vivino head-on.

**Winery-visit / wine-tourism cluster (fragmented, separate from the rating apps):**
- **Winery Passport** — the leading consumer visit-tracker: stamp 6,500+ wineries, 22 badges, log notes/photos. *Closest existing competitor to a tasting-room logging app.*

**Gaps Cork & Note can exploit:**
- **(a) Winery-visit logging — STRONG, underserved gap (high confidence).** All five majors are absent here, and the existing visit-trackers are regionally siloed and disconnected from personal cellars/ratings/AI. The opportunity is to **unify** visit logging (stamps/badges/itineraries) + personal tasting journal + at-winery purchases + social sharing.

Cork & Note's differentiator is an AI somm that reasons over **the user's actual visits + tastings + cellar + travel context** ("build me a Napa tasting-room itinerary, then a buy list") — a combination **no competitor currently offers**.

## 3. Monetization Models — Survey & Recommendation

### 3.1 Subscription / Freemium — **recommended primary**

**Competitor price points (confidence medium; some pages 403'd, verify in-app):**
- Vivino Premium: ~$4.99/mo promo; ~$29.99/yr regional (US annual conflicts $29.99 vs ~$50).
- Wine-Searcher PRO: **$10.99/mo**.
- CellarTracker: pay-what-you-want, **floor ~$40/yr** scaled by cellar size.
- Delectable Premium: **$5.99/mo** (a $1.99 tier also cited; pricing may be dated).

**What competitors gate (so you know what's "premium-worthy"):** exact match/recommendation scores, wine-list/menu scanning, drink windows, food pairings, **ad-free**, full price history & alerts, cellar valuation, professional critic reviews.

**Realistic conversion benchmarks (no wine-specific public data exists — these are general consumer/mobile benchmarks, applied by inference):**
- Freemium free-to-paid typically **1–10%, clustering 2–5%** (CrazyEgg, Lenny's Newsletter).
- **RevenueCat 2025:** median download-to-paid **~2.18% for pure freemium** vs **~12.11% for hard paywalls** — gating harder dramatically lifts conversion.
- Lower-priced plans convert trials better (47.8% vs 28.4%) but yield lower LTV (~$8 vs ~$55). A wine app sits in the low-ARPU "Social & Lifestyle" band.

**Recommendation:** Launch a **soft freemium** model. Keep core logging, the winery map, basic browsing, and a metered amount of AI-sommelier chat **free** (you need volume/UGC and the AI is your hook). Gate **Premium (~$4.99/mo or ~$29.99–39.99/yr)** behind: unlimited AI sommelier, itinerary planning, cellar value/insights, advanced filters/exports, ad-free, and premium badges/stats. Plan for **~2–5% conversion**; test a harder paywall on the AI feature to push toward the higher band. **Consider a one-time "lifetime unlock" SKU** too — ~40% of Social & Lifestyle apps now blend subscriptions with one-time/lifetime purchases, and some users pay $30 once who'll never pay $5/mo.

### 3.2 B2B winery / tasting-room partnerships — **recommended secondary (later)**

This monetizes your visit-logging wedge and faces an audience that's actively distressed: Napa visits −18% (2025), Sonoma −14%→−8%, tasting fees up 15–44%. Wineries already pay **$79–1,249/mo + ~1.5–2% txn fees** for DTC software (Commerce7, WineDirect) — they have budget and a desperate need for foot-traffic/engagement tools.
- **Models:** sponsored/featured map placements (Hello Vino's CPC model), featured winery listings, **post-visit re-engagement** (a winery's #1 problem), and **visit analytics** (who visited, what they logged, conversion to club sign-ups).
- Note: <9% of wineries use text marketing but those who do see ~99% open rates — tasting rooms are a hugely underexploited, high-intent surface you can help them capture.
- **Why secondary, not primary:** B2B sales is high-touch and slow; you need consumer scale first to have something wineries will pay for. Build the consumer base, *then* sell the audience back to wineries.

### 3.3 Affiliate / commerce ("buy this wine") — **defer; pursue cautiously at scale**
- **Margins are thin downstream:** US wine retailer affiliate payouts run **~2.5–6%** (Wine.com up to ~4%/2.5%; rates conflict across aggregators). The rich position is being the *marketplace* taking the ~10–20% merchant fee (Vivino) — but that requires licensing infrastructure and scale.
- **⚠️ Drizly is dead** (Uber shut it down end of March 2024) — exclude it from any plan; aggregator pages listing it are stale.
- **Legal weight (see §5):** three-tier/tied-house rules and state-by-state DTC make alcohol commerce genuinely complex. Affiliate commissions can even implicate "inducement" rules — needs alcohol-beverage counsel.
- **If/when you do it:** route orders to **state-licensed local retailers** who hold the license and fulfill (the Vivino/Uber model — the app never takes title or ships). Until then, a simple "find this wine near you / at this winery" link is lower-risk than a transactional cart.

### 3.4 Advertising / sponsorship — **don't lead with it**
- Banner eCPMs in the US are **<$1** — effectively worthless at your scale. Lifestyle interstitials/rewarded video are higher ($8–20+) but only matter at large DAU.
- **Better play:** sell "ad-free" as a Premium perk (every major does), and pursue *direct winery sponsored placements* as part of the B2B line (§3.2) rather than ad-network inventory.

### 3.5 In-app purchases / one-time unlocks — **yes, as a complement**
- IAP hit ~$150B in 2024 (~50% of mobile revenue); one-time/lifetime plans grew from 6.4%→10.3% of plan share (2023→2025); **35% of apps now blend subscription + one-time**, led by Social & Lifestyle (39.4%).
- **Candidate SKUs:** lifetime ad-removal, premium content/region packs (e.g., curated tasting-room itineraries), cellar-valuation unlock.

### Recommended monetization stack (summary)
1. **Primary:** Freemium subscription (Premium ~$4.99/mo · ~$29.99–39.99/yr), AI sommelier as the headline gated feature.
2. **Complement:** one-time "lifetime" unlock SKU.
3. **Secondary (post-scale):** B2B winery sponsored placements + visit analytics.
4. **Tertiary (defer, counsel-gated):** licensed-retailer commerce / "buy this wine."
5. **Not a primary:** ad networks — sell "ad-free" as a perk instead.

## 4. Market Context (US, 2023–2026)

**Bottom line:** the wine *category* is shrinking; the wine-*tech* category is growing. Position for the latter.

**Size & growth (vendor figures = low confidence; conflict 2–10×):**
- US wine market ~**$75–80B** (2024–25). The credible near-term read (SVB 2026) is **flat-to-down** through 2027–2028 — *not* the 6–9% growth report-sellers imply.
- US **wine tourism (high confidence — WineAmerica 2025): 74M visits, ~$14.13B annual tourism spend**; California alone 25.2M visits / ~$8.6B. This is the spend pool your visit-logging wedge sits next to.
- Wine *e-commerce / wine-tech* consistently projected to grow **11–34% CAGR** (vendor numbers, directional) — multiples faster than wine volume.

**Trends that matter for your positioning:**
- **DTC wine shipping is a ~$4B channel but contracting** (2024 volume −10%; 2025 mid-year −12%) while premiumizing (avg bottle $52.68). Wineries are anxious to defend it — supports the B2B engagement angle.
- **Tasting-room traffic is down** (Napa −18% in 2025) and fees are up — wineries need tools to attract, convert, and *re-engage* visitors. Direct product wedge, but also a headwind (fewer feet through the door).
- **Younger drinkers drink less** (Gallup 2025: only 54% of adults drink, lowest in ~90 yrs; 18–34 down to 50%). *But* millennials are now the **largest US wine-drinking cohort (31%)** and choose wine via **recommendations/influencers/"vibe"** over critic scores — favorable for a social, casual-friendly discovery app. Cause of the decline is contested (wellness vs. affordability).
- **Premiumization is stalling** (IWSR 2025: wine value −4%) but value is still migrating to the $15–30 band — favors curation/discovery.
- **AI shopping is mainstreaming** (~70% of shoppers have used an AI tool for shopping; AI sommelier is an active, fundable theme) — tailwind for your AI feature, though incumbents already ship AI.

**Bear case (be honest):** structural consumption decline (52+ negative months; OIV: US −5.8% in 2024), generational replacement failure (SVB: 3–4 more years of pain), health/"neo-prohibition" (Jan 2025 Surgeon General cancer advisory), tariffs raising import prices, and softening tasting-room traffic. **Strategic risk = Vivino's incumbency + a shrinking, health-conscious, price-sensitive drinker base.** Mitigate by staying narrow and differentiated.

## 5. Regulatory & Platform Constraints (US)

> Several official pages (Google Play, Apple Ads, TikTok Ads, Sovos) block automated fetching; quoted wording should be re-confirmed in a browser. App-store rules are high-confidence; alcohol-commerce/tied-house law is the murkiest area and needs **alcohol-beverage counsel** before any commerce launch.

**App stores — alcohol apps are allowed (apps that merely *facilitate* alcohol sales are permitted; Vivino/Uber ship today):**
- **Apple:** Guideline **1.4.3** bars apps that *encourage excessive consumption* or market to minors and bars facilitating sale of *controlled substances/tobacco* — **alcohol is not on that list**, so alcohol-sales facilitation is allowed. Declaring alcohol content yields a **17+ rating** (migrating to a new 16+/18+ band system). Use a **declared-age** age gate (1.2.1(a)/4.7.5); **geo-restrict** any commerce to states where partners are licensed (the 5.1.4(a) cannabis analogy).
- **Google Play:** answer "yes" to **Age-Restricted Physical Goods** if the app focuses on alcohol sale; can enable **Restrict Minor Access**; IARC questionnaire yields a mature rating. Alcohol sales not flatly prohibited (unlike tobacco/nicotine).

**Advertising alcohol (every network: age-restrict, geo-comply, no minors in creative, no excessive-consumption messaging):**
- **Apple Search Ads / Google Ads:** allowed with age/geo targeting; online-sale promotion restricted in some locales.
- **Meta:** US ads must **target 21+**.
- **⚠️ TikTok:** opened US alcohol *brand* ads in Aug 2024 (audience must be ~73.8%+ legal age) **but bans alcohol e-commerce/delivery ads** — so you can run *awareness* ads but **not** ads pushing wine purchase/delivery.

**Age-gating:** App-store *download* gating is 17+/18+, but US alcohol *purchase* gating must be **21+** — implement both. Layer a neutral declared-age gate at entry; reserve hard ID verification for any actual point-of-sale/checkout (where the licensed retailer bears the legal duty).

**Affiliate / FTC:** any material connection must be disclosed **"clearly and conspicuously"** (16 CFR Part 255; 2023 update). "Paid link" next to the link is adequate; "affiliate link" alone or buried disclosures are not. Alcohol adds **tied-house/inducement** complexity (TTB 27 CFR Part 6 + state ABC) — structure any commissions carefully with counsel.

**State DTC wine (the binding constraint for commerce):** winery DTC is legal in nearly all states (exceptions ~Utah, Delaware; RI/MS restrictive) but **retailer DTC** (more relevant to a marketplace) is permitted in only ~a dozen-plus states and **changes yearly** (Sovos updates annually). The compliant path: be a **technology/marketing intermediary** that never takes title or ships; route orders to **state-licensed local retailers**.

**Data privacy:** minimize DOB/ID/biometric retention; **Illinois BIPA** (private right of action) is the top exposure for ID+selfie verification; age and **precise geolocation** are "sensitive personal information" under CCPA/CPRA + ~19 state laws — and you *need* geolocation for both the winery map and any state-by-state commerce legality. Explicit consent screens + a privacy policy enumerating age/location/biometric collection.

## 6. Marketing & User Acquisition Plan (Small Budget)

**Why it's viable:** millennials are the largest US wine cohort and choose by recommendation/"vibe"; 40% of US wine consumers use TikTok weekly; ASO (free) is ~65% of app discovery.

**Prioritized channels:**

| Priority | Channel | Cost | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **ASO** | $0–80/mo tooling | ~65% of discovery is store search. Win long-tail keywords ("winery tasting room," "wine tasting notes," "scan wine label," "wine cellar tracker"). Top-3 keyword rank ≈ 4.6× the installs of rank 8–15. Tools: AppDrift (free), Applyra (~$10/mo). |
| 2 | **Founder-led #WineTok / Reels** | Time | Casual, human, educational content with the app shown naturally ("scanning every wine at the tasting room," "3 grocery wines under $15"). Creator-led content drives ~70% higher CTR. |
| 3 | **Tasting-room QR partnerships** | Time + materials | **Your defensible edge.** QR table-tents/cards → save tasting notes + install. Tasting rooms are high-intent and underexploited (<9% of wineries even use text marketing). Beat Vivino on the visitor segment. |
| 4 | **Apple Search Ads** | $50/day | Cheapest, highest-retention iOS install source (~$1.40–3.00 CPI). Brand + high-intent keywords. Start here before broad Meta/Google. |
| 5 | **Micro/nano wine influencers** | Gifted + small fees | 5–15 creators with authentic casual audiences; track with promo codes; favor long-term over one-off. Micros: ~$150–500/IG post, ~$200–800/TikTok; ~61% of brands report higher ROI from micros. |
| 6 | **Community (Reddit/FB/Discord)** | Time | r/wine etc.; 90/10 value-to-promo rule, build karma first, founder transparency. Slow but high-trust. |
| 7 | **Email waitlist + PR** | $0–low | Pre-launch waitlist (welcome emails 50–60% open); press kit to wine/lifestyle + local winery-region media (not just tech press). Product Hunt is harder now (~10% get featured). |
| 8 | **Referral loop** | Dev time | Double-sided rewards (both get a perk); shareable "my tasting note / cellar / winery trip." Target K≈0.2–0.3 — a helper, not the engine. |

**Paid CPI benchmarks (US, 2024–26; ranges — validate with your own tests):** Apple Search Ads ~$1.40–3.00 · TikTok ~$2.88 · Meta ~$3.50–3.75 · Google UAC ~$1–5. iOS CPI rising. **Start with ASA $50/day + a $500–1,000 TikTok test on your best organic creative; hold broad Meta/Google until you know LTV.**

**KPIs to track:** retention **D1 ~25% / D7 ~10–13% / D30 ~5–7%** (set iOS targets conservatively; wine usage is occasion-based so DAU/MAU will be lower than social apps); CAC by channel with **LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1**; funnel (store CR → signup → activation = first tasting note/scan → free→paid); ARPU, churn, k-factor, payback period.

**Phased go-to-market:**
- **Pre-launch (~2–4 mo out):** lock ASO; build landing page + email waitlist; start founder #WineTok + authentic Reddit presence; line up 5–15 micro creators and 2–5 tasting-room QR partners; prep press kit + Product Hunt teaser.
- **Launch week:** coordinated waitlist emails + Product Hunt + creator posts + press, same window; activate tasting-room QR codes; turn on double-sided referral; ASA $50/day on brand + high-intent keywords.
- **Growth:** double down on best CAC/retention channel (likely ASA + organic TikTok + tasting-room QR); add a small TikTok ad test on winning creative; expand SEO clusters; scale winery/wine-club partnerships; iterate referral toward K≈0.2–0.3; onboarding email automation for retention; continuous ASO A/B testing; reinvest by LTV:CAC.

## 7. Recommended Path (TL;DR)

1. **Position narrow:** the wine app for **casual drinkers + winery visitors** — "log your tasting-room visits and your cellar, with an AI sommelier that knows what you've actually tasted." Don't fight Vivino head-on.
2. **Lead the product with the visit-logging + AI-sommelier combo** — the one thing no incumbent offers.
3. **Monetize via freemium subscription** (~$4.99/mo · ~$29.99–39.99/yr), AI sommelier as the headline gated feature; add a one-time lifetime unlock. Expect ~2–5% conversion.
4. **Layer B2B winery partnerships** (sponsored placements + visit analytics) once you have consumer scale.
5. **Defer alcohol commerce** until you have scale and counsel; if you add "find this wine," route to licensed retailers and never take title.
6. **Grow on a shoestring with ASO + founder #WineTok + tasting-room QR partnerships**, then layer Apple Search Ads and micro-influencers once early retention/LTV looks healthy.
7. **Respect the rules:** 17+/18+ store rating but 21+ for any purchase; declared-age gate; geo-restrict commerce; FTC "paid link" disclosures; minimize age/location/biometric data.
